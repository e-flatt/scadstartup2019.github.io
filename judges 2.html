Judging Criteria

Product Thinking / Feasibility / Impact

How practically viable the concept is in terms of feasibility, idea development, and time. The concept must also address and target a real user need in its execution. The research conducted through the week has a tangible influence on the final concept. How well and uniquely the concept addresses the theme.

Craft and Execution

Is it a quality design visually? Do the interactions feel natural and intuitive? Is there appropriate context and branding?

Presentation and Communication

How compelling and powerful is the presentation? How does effective storytelling help craft the narrative of both the concept and the process? Does it appear as each major and team member has a strong voice and impact?

Wow Factor

How novel and unique is the idea? Does a similar product already exist in the world today? What other deliverables did the team use to go above and beyond in their execution (posters, physical prototypes, etc)?

StartUp Rules:

You must agree to the rules, regulations, and expectations of etiquette for StartUp 2019. You'll agree to allow the StartUp Team to thoroughly review your final deliverables and process work to ensure honesty, integrity, and the absence of Intellectual property violations. You admit that all work produced is completely, and solely the product of your team unless explicitly stated in a written agreement from the Startup Team. Cheating is not allowed in any way shape or form.

Any projects / solutions created for class, work or personal use, currently or previously, are ineligible for use in StartUp 2019.

Any work, to any degree, created by a student outside of the team will result in disqualification.

Any projects or concepts created for Global Game Jam are ineligible for submission.
